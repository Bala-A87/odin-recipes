Paneer Butter Masala

Description

Paneer butter masala is a rich and creamy curry made with paneer, spices, onions, tomatoes, cashews, and butter. As the name suggests, the curry is cooked in butter, which imparts its characteristic buttery flavor to the dish.

To make paneer butter masala, firstly onions, tomatoes and cashews are sauteed with spices. These are then blended to a silky smooth puree forming the curry base. This is further simmered with butter and spices to make the butter masala. Lastly, cubed paneer is added to it along with cream, which makes it creamy and amazingly delicious!

Ingredients

  • Oil - 1 tbsp
  • [Optional] Green cardamoms - 4 nos
  • Onions, chopped - 1 large, 120g/1 cup
  • Tomatoes, finely chopped - 3 medium, 350g/1.5-2 cups
  • Salt - to taste
  • [Optional] Sugar - 0.75 tsp
  • Cashew, whole - 15 nos
  • Kashmiri red chilli powder - 0.75-1 tsp
  • Garam masala - 0.75-1 tsp
  • [Optional] Coriander powder - 1 tsp
  • Water - 1.5 cups
  • Bay leaf - 1 small
  • [Optional] Cinnamon - 0.5 inch piece
  • [Optional] Clove - 3 nos
  • Butter - 1.5-2 tbsp
  • Ginger garlic paste - 1.5 tsp
  • Paneer, diced - 200-250g/2 heaped cups
  • Kasuri methi - 1 tsp
  • Cream, heavy/whipping/cooking - 3 tbsp
  • Coriander leaves - 2 tbsp

Steps

  1. Heat oil in a pan. Add 2 green cardamoms and the onions. Stir fry until they turn transparent to light pink. Add ginger garlic paste.
  2. Add tomatoes and salt. Saute for 3 minutes. Cover and cook until the onions and tomatoes turn soft and completely mushy.
  3. Add garam masala, coriander powder, cashews, red chilli powder, and sugar.
  4. Stir fry until the onion tomato masala turns aromatic. This takes 2-3 minutes.
  5. Turn off the stove and cool completely.
  6. Transfer to a blender and pour 1 cup water. Blend to a really silky smooth puree. If it is not smooth, filter it through a strainer to another bowl. Set this aside.
  7. Heat butter in the same pan. When the butter melts, add cinnamons, cloves, green cardamoms, and bay leaf.
  8. Reduce the flame to low, saute ginger garlic paste for a minute until it turns aromatic.
  9. Stir in the blended onion tomato puree. Taste test you puree and optionally add more red chilli powder for a deeper color.
  10. Pour more water and mix well. Bring it to a rolling boil. Reduce the heat to medium and cook covered until the gravy cooks down to a thicker consistency. Keep stirring every few minutes.
  11. You know the gravy is done when it turns thick and traces of fat is visible on the gravy. Taste test and add more salt or garam masala if needed.
  12. Lower the flame completely, add cubed paneer. Take kasuri methi in your palm, crush it and sprinkle.
  13. Mix, and cook covered on low for 2 minutes. Turn off and pour some cream and stir.
  14. Transfer the paneer butter masala to a serving bowl. Garnish with more cream and chopped coriander leaves.
