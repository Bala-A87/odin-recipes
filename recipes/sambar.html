Sambar

Description

Sambar is a South Indian lentil and vegetable stew made with pigeon pea lentils, tamarind and a unique spice blend called sambar powder.

Ingredients

  • Tuvar Dal / Split Pigeon Pea - 1/3 cup
  • Water
  • oil - 4 tbsp
  • Fenugreek - 1/2 tsp
  • Cumin Seeds - 1 tbsp
  • Rice - 1/2 tbsp
  • coriander seeds - 1 tbsp
  • Chana Dal - 1/2 tbsp
  • Cloves - 2 small
  • Green Cardamom - 1 small
  • Hing / Asafoetida - 1/2 tsp
  • garlic - 5-6 cloves optional
  • Ginger - 1/2 inch optional
  • Coconut - 2 tbsp chopped
  • Curry Leaves - 5-6 sprigs
  • Whole Red Chili - 9-11 small
  • Haldi / Turmeric - 1/2 tsp
  • Kashmiri Red Chili Powder - 1 tsp
  • Onion - 1 large sliced
  • salt - 1 1/2 tsp
  • Green Chili - 2 small roughly chopped
  • Assorted Vegetables - 1 cup roughly chopped Bottle Gourd, Raddish and Tomato or Eggplant, drumsticks, ladyfingers
  • Tamarind pulp - 2-3 tbsp. If store bought tamarind paste then use 1 tbsp
  • Jaggery - 1-2 tbsp or sugar
  • Mustard Seeds - 1 tsp

Steps

  1. Soak the tuvar dal in water for 1 hour or until they double in volume.
  2. In a pan, heat the oil on medium flame. Add cumin seeds, methi seeds, chana dal, rice, coriander seeds, hing, cloves, green cardamom, ginger and garlic, coconut and sprig of curry leaves. Fry for 2 minutes on medium flame until they start to pop.
  3. Once the masala starts to pop, add red chili, cook for 2-3 minutes over low flame.
  4. Add haldi, Kashmiri red chili, onion and salt. Cook for 2-3 minutes over low flame.
  5. Once done allow the masala to cool down completely. Place the masala along with some water, in a mixer jar and blend everything to make a smooth paste. Set aside.
  6. In the pressure cooker, pour oil and add green chili, mixed vegetables and salt. Cook the vegetables for 2 minutes over high heat.
  7. Then add the masala, and give it a good mix. Cook for 1 minute for high flame.
  8. Add the soaked tuvar dal along with the water, mix well and bring it to a boil.
  9. Once it comes to a rapid boil, place the lid and cook the tuvar dal over high heat for about 5 whistles, after that lower the flame and cook for further 15 minutes. Once done allow the pressure cooker to naturally cool down.
  10. Once cooled open the lid and again bring the sambar to a boil over high heat.
  11. Add the tamarind paste and jaggery and cook for 3-4 minutes in high heat. This will bring out the sweet, spicy and tangy flavour in the sambar.
  12. In a small tadka pan, pour oil and then add the mustard seeds, allow it to crackle.
  13. Then add the whole red chilies and hing, mix and finally add the curry leaves.
  14. Add this tadka to the sambar and give it a final mix. Sambar is ready.
